01 — Overview

The Challenge

Helios Energy needed a real-time monitoring interface capable of displaying thousands of sensor readings per second across a tokamak plasma containment system — while remaining readable under extreme cognitive load during ignition sequences.

01
Real-time Data
12,000+ sensor readings per second rendered without frame drops across six display zones.
02
Operator Safety
Critical alerts required a sub-200ms response pipeline from plasma anomaly to operator notification.
03
Zero Tolerance
Interface errors during an ignition event could result in uncontrolled plasma disruption — design had to be flawless.
02 — Roadmap

Nine Months,
One Console

From discovery through live deployment in the Helios Mk-IV test facility. Each sprint refined the information hierarchy around plasma physicists' actual mental models — not assumed ones.

Phase 01 · Months 1–2
Discovery & Ethnography
Shadowed operators during 6 ignition sequences. Identified 23 failure modes in the legacy interface through contextual inquiry.
Phase 02 · Months 3–4
System Architecture
Defined the information hierarchy across five core modules: containment, heating, diagnostics, vacuum, and cryogenics.
Phase 03 · Months 5–7
Interface Design
120+ screens across high-fidelity prototypes. Validated with operators in simulated ignition drills before any development began.
Phase 04 · Months 8–9
Deployment & Tuning
Embedded with the engineering team during Mk-IV integration. Iterated on alert thresholds and data density in real reactor conditions.
